Sweetwater Chair

One of four designs selected from an entire Auburn ID class for full-scale production. Built in steel and wood. Deployed at Old Majestic Brewing Company. The scale model fit in the cup holder.

Traditional Adirondack chairs sit you low, tilt you back 15 degrees, and make standing back up a project. After 20 minutes your head falls onto the headrest and talking to the person next to you is a neck workout. The Sweetwater Chair pushes against both problems — raised seat height, slight forward tilt, A-frame leg geometry to make the load path work.

The brief was a complete outdoor entertainment collection for a brewery venue: chair, octagonal fire pit table with a lava rock insert, park bench, and side table. The whole studio built to it. Out of the whole class, four chair designs were selected for full-scale production. This one was one of them.

Full scale meant real fabrication. The process ran SolidWorks → KeyShot rendering → Illustrator fabrication drawing at 1:1 scale → full-size print on the welding table → steel tube cut to compound angles (22°/16°/18°) → tack and weld → powder coat black → laser etch the Sweetwater logo into the mahogany backrest → bolt the stained boards to the frame. The completed chair was installed at Old Majestic Brewing Company in Auburn.

After the class project, the design kept going. A personal version — same geometry, mahogany boards, "M" monogram laser etched into the back — lives on a lake dock in Alabama.
