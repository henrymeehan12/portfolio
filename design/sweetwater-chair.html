Sweetwater Chair

Adirondack, reconsidered. Raised to help elderly and disabled users exit. Tilted forward so you can talk to the person next to you without your head against the headrest.

Role
Student designer
Typology
Outdoor hospitality furniture
Tools
Sketch · Scale models · CAD (tube structure)
Year
Auburn

Traditional Adirondack chairs are deep, low, and tilted back about 15 degrees. Great for 20 minutes. After that you're tired — your head keeps falling back onto the headrest, which means talking to anyone next to you is a neck workout. And if you're older or have limited mobility, getting out of one is a whole thing.

The Sweetwater Chair was a simple push against those problems. Raise the seat so your feet can get under you to stand up. Tilt the whole chair forward to take the load off your neck and open up the posture for conversation. Keep the silhouette familiar — still recognizably the chair you already know.

It's laser-etchable too, so any bar or brewery can brand one in a pass. Sketched the form, tested it in scale models, validated the tube structure in CAD, built it.
