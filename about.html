About

Henry Meehan

I grew up fishing in Alabama. When I was 15, I spent time in the Maasai Mara learning under Peter Blackwell — a professional wildlife photographer and bush guide — and got my first real look at what a camera can do. I didn't fully take it seriously until college, when industrial design school taught me how to see things: form, light, composition, what makes an object worth looking at.

In 2017 I went on my first fly fishing trip to Montana with Lillard Fly Fishing Expeditions. I went back every summer. In 2019 Will Lillard asked if I'd guide for him. I guided every summer until 2025.

In spring 2023 I built the Relic Messenger — a guitar I designed and built from scratch, exhibited at the Gibson Garage in Nashville. I graduated Auburn in May of that year. That summer I went back to Montana to guide. That fall I planned a trip: New Zealand, Fiji, Singapore, Thailand, Vietnam, Japan. Four months. I came back in April 2024.

I went back to Montana for one more season, then drove south through Utah in October. Bonneville Salt Flats. Corona Arch. Big Bend. Started at DSW in April 2025.

I'm a product engineer now — designing parts for 18-wheelers, tractors, data centers, industrial equipment. On the side I'm building NEXFORM, an AI system that reads engineering drawings and builds 3D CAD models automatically. It's the hardest thing I've tried to make. I'm still working on it.

Based in Birmingham, Alabama.
