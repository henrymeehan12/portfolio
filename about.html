About

Henry Meehan

I'm a product engineer in metal fabrication — designing parts for 18-wheelers, tractors, data centers, and industrial equipment. The work involves understanding how things get made before they exist, which turns out to be the same skill as photography.

I studied industrial design, which gave me the habit of drawing before building and thinking about material before picking it up. That same instinct ended up in the camera — I tend to look for structure, texture, and the moment right before something changes.

The fishing started in Alabama and never stopped. New Zealand in 2024 was a back-country fly fishing expedition on the South Island — rivers so clear and cold they didn't seem real. The Ha Giang Loop in northern Vietnam was a motorbike trip through karst mountains I wasn't prepared for. Kenya was the Maasai Mara in dry season, which speaks for itself.

On the side I'm building NEXFORM — an AI system that reads engineering drawings and builds 3D CAD models automatically. Ten years of manufacturing knowledge, turned into software.

Based in Birmingham, Alabama.
